Science and math

##### Implemetation of 3 abstract concepts 1. Provide information 2. Process information 3. Receive information
##### Tweet 1. Provide text to a tweet 2. Twitter parses, ensures length compliance, checks for abuse, publishes, and then might even announce it 3. You see confirmation that your tweet has been posted
##### Credit card purchase 1. Physically swipe card thus providing your unique id (credit card #); specify amount 2. Visa through its network approves or declines the request based on credit of the requestor, other risks 3. You are approved or declined
##### These two things are same in terms of the mathematical representation 1. They feel different because they have different economic impacts on us 2. But's only because we attach utility to these things 3. If tomrrow, the issue of hunger, learning, shelter were solved, then we would value communication much more 4. A world that values tweets (social interaction) more than money, is not entirely inconceivable 5. *The math remains the same*
### If you disagree, we should stop and discuss
##### To do all these things, the institution needs 1. Your unique id. They need to know who you are. 2. Prior history. 3. Means of communication.
##### Unique id - There is a unique id for virtually anything you consume - The provider of this unique id has information and that information is stored - The cost of switching ids is costly
##### Prior history - Your prior history is either kept as a series of records that are interconnected - Or, captured as a single unit - think of your credit score, or the check mark on your Twitter account - Typically, information (from the perspective of the provider) is not lost
##### Institutions already use a chain - It consists of relational databases - It is hierarchical (there is a reason why organizations have turf wars) - It is constantly being updated *Sound familiar?*
##### Means of communication - Digital - Physical
##### Blockchain transforms these principles 1. There are unique ids. But they cannot be tied to you. 2. Information is encoded in blocks of time; transactions are related but not in a hierarchical way 3. Still need some form of digital connection
